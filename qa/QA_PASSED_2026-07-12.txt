8 findings confirmed and fixed as executable artefacts:

  1. BUG (accessibility) -- the heatmap's single most decisive cell (+36.4, WB life
     expectancy) measured ~3.9:1 text contrast, below the project's 4.5:1 mandate.
     Independently re-derived the WCAG math and found NEITHER black nor white text
     passes 4.5:1 against this specific background colour -- a colour-branching fix
     could not have worked. Fixed with a white stroke-halo behind the text
     (paint-order="stroke fill"), which guarantees legibility against any background.
  2. MODERATE-HIGH (false claim) -- the order-effect chart's caption asserted AICc
     near-tie/decisive status predicts forecast-magnitude impact; independently
     verified against the raw CSV that 6+ of 21 series directly contradict this.
     Recoloured the chart by its own >=2% threshold (matching the manuscript's own
     language) and rewrote the caption to state the true, more interesting finding:
     the two are only loosely coupled -- which is exactly why the manuscript presents
     them as two separate figures.
  3. CRITICAL (test coverage) -- 3-way independent confirmation (self-QA + 2 advisors):
     zero regression tests existed for the 5 new chart functions. Added 4 new tests,
     each independently recomputing the expected value from source CSVs and asserting
     it appears correctly in the shipped HTML -- not just "the file exists."
  4. MAJOR -- the method-comparison chart's log-scale y-axis was disclosed only in 9px
     in-SVG text, not the prose caption. Added an explicit warning to the caption.
  5. LOW -- LSTM error-bar rescaling used ARIMA's own MAPE/MAE ratio for both LSTM
     configs instead of each config's own ratio (tiny numeric effect, real
     methodological imprecision). Fixed to derive each method's own factor.
  6. MODERATE -- README's and the Dashboard's <h1> both truncated the manuscript's full
     registered title. Fixed both; Poster was not flagged (already recovers the missing
     clause as a subtitle) and was left untouched per the user's "poster is perfect"
     verdict from the prior turn.
  7. LOW-MODERATE -- main.tex's ORCID was a LaTeX comment, never rendered into the
     compiled frontmatter (the docx build was unaffected -- it hardcodes the ORCID
     independently). Fixed by folding it into \address{}, guaranteed to render without
     depending on an ORCID-specific macro this never-compile-tested LaTeX may lack.
  8. MODERATE -- CI Python-version mismatch (self-QA, both advisors independently
     confirmed). Fixed: CI now installs 3.13, matching the claimed reproduction
     environment.

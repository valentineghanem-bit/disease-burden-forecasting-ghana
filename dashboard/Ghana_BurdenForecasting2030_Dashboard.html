Structural-break sensitivity: 2020 COVID-19 vs. 2022 currency crisis, all 21 series

AICc improvement from adding a level-shift dummy at each candidate break, vs. the no-intervention specification (manuscript Table 5). Orange = AICc favours a break; blue = it does not; grey "NT" = not testable (fewer than 2 post-break or 5 pre-break observations). Bold, asterisked cells (Δ>2) are this paper's operational "decisive" threshold (Methods) -- a low bar, not a formal significance test. Hover any cell for the exact ΔAICc and post-break observation count. Testing rather than assuming away these breaks: a decisive currency-crisis break shifts the malaria 2030 forecast by a further +26.5% (larger than the order-selection effect above), and a decisive break at both candidate dates is found for the World Bank life-expectancy series -- a plausible partial explanation for its divergence from the WHO-GHO life-expectancy series, not a resolved causal account. Most series' break tests are underpowered on 2–5 post-break observations.

Why the malaria ARIMA-vs-ETS gap narrows: scale, not order

Isolated on a like-for-like basis: fitting on the log scale (this paper's protocol for non-negative-bounded series) closes most of the gap on its own; adding the series' own AICc-selected order on top does not close it further. Hover a bar for the exact gap at each stage.
